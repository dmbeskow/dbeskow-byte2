Discussion of Data Quality

Brief Summary of the the quality of the data is presented here

Is the data complete?

The data is a nearly complete data set of all Twitter data that contains the "place" attribute that is within 100 miles of the Pyeong Chang Olympic Stadium for the time period of interest. Note that this includes both those users that have geolocation on, as well as those users that allow Twitter to generally infer their location. The plot below provides a view of the spatial and temporal distribution of the data. It also provides the distribution across languages.

Is the data coherent?

Overall the distributions matched our expectations after taking it all into consideration. At first it seemed like we had a high volume of non-olympic "noise" in the data. After plotting the map above, however, we realized that our radius was too big, and we were picking up alot of the normal Twitter 'chatter' coming out of Seoul, Korea. In later iterations of this we intend to shrink our radius in order to eliminate this noise.

Is the data correct?

The data is accurate and correct, as long as you understand the underlying population usage and the collection technique described above. Any use of this data cannot be used to provide statistical inference on the entire Korean Population, or on the entire population of visitors watching the games, since Twitter users are not randomly "sprinkled" over either population. That being said, this does provide a great source of data to understand the conversation that is originating and emerging from right around the Olympic Games.
